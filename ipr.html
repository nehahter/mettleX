Understanding Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

Intellectual Property (IP) refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions, designs, brand names, artwork, or software, that are protected by law. IPR includes patents, copyrights, trademarks, and trade secrets, which grant you exclusive rights to use, produce, or sell your innovation. Protecting your IP is crucial for preventing unauthorized use, copying, or exploitation by others.
